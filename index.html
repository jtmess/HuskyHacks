About Husky Hacks

Hackathon: an event in which computer programmers and others involved in software development and hardware development, including graphic designers, interface designers and project managers, collaborate intensively.

HuskyHacks is Northeastern University's first 24-hour CCIS hackathon. Over 100 students will form teams and have from the afternoon of April 2 to the evening of April 3 to build something amazing. The possibilities are limitless; learn a new language, power on your Particle for the first time, try on an Oculus Rift, and turn your dreams into reality. Students will be allowed to innovate using any software or hardware tools, as long as they are working on a fresh project. No previously started projects will be allowed. Over 100 HuskyHacks participants will be chosen based on application, and the event will be held in West Village H April 2-3.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is it?

HuskyHacks will take place April 2-3 2016 from 6:00pm to 4:00pm.

How much does it cost?

HuskyHacks is completely free and includes free food, drinks, and swag.

What should I bring?

All you need is your student ID, a laptop+charger, and any hardware you want to hack on.

Who can come?

HuskyHacks this semester is open to any undergraduates at Northeastern. Fear not, you will be able to invite your friends from other schools in the fall.

Do I have to know how to code?

Absolutely not! A hackathon is the perfect place and time to learn. There will be mentors available to ease you through your first hackathon experience.

How do teams work?

If you already have teammates in mind, list their emails in your application and you will be considered as a team.

Will there be hardware?

We are working to secure hardware, and participants are welcome to bring their own hardware to hack on.

What can I win?

Money and Arduinos and stuff. But is that really why you go to hackathons?
